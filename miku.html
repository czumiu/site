concert visuals

Visuals created for a recurring Hatsune Miku concert series at the University of Houston.
Short procedural transition animations built in Godot as real time systems, then rendered out for use in a Premiere edit to ensure stable playback.

Helix

Procedural transition used to mask a mid performance model swap between Miku costumes. Multiple helical strips and particle layers sweep vertically across the frame, creating full screen coverage to hide the change.

Geometric Outro

Outro with two stages. It begins with rotating polygon structures and layered line patterns that expand outward in a controlled sequence.
It transitions to a flare phase that breaks the rigid structure with additive trails and light based motion, shifting the system from ordered geometry into accumulation and dispersion.

Flare Outro

Outro built from sequential flare emitters that pass through the frame to carry motion between segments.
Each emitter generates a continuous particle trail that is distorted over time, allowing the paths to evolve rather than follow fixed curves. The final image is built through layered emission and accumulation instead of static geometry.
